To the Interledger Foundation Fellowship Selection Committee,

I write in support of Diego Torres Lopez's application to the 2026 Interledger Fellowship Program.

As Head of the Teaching, Research and Innovation Unit at the Corporació de Salut del Maresme i la Selva (CSMS), a public healthcare organisation serving the Alt Maresme and Selva Marítima regions through Hospital de Calella and Hospital de Blanes, I have been in direct dialogue with Diego on research and innovation initiatives at the intersection of AI, health, and digital inclusion. That vantage point lets me speak to two things the Fellowship selection committee will want to assess: whether Diego can actually do rigorous work in a healthcare and digital-inclusion context, and whether his proposal is grounded in real relationships and real deployments, not just good intentions.

**On technical rigour in clinical and research settings.** My own work includes developing AI algorithms for cardiovascular risk prediction in patients with familial hypercholesterolemia, published in peer-reviewed venues such as *European Heart Journal, Digital Health*. I'm familiar with what it takes to produce defensible, reproducible technical work in a healthcare context. Diego's product Contestia (a WhatsApp AI receptionist running in production in Spanish health clinics) is an example of exactly that: a real system, in production, with paying customers, honest post-mortems, and an attention to data protection, clinical context, and patient experience that many developers skip. That discipline translates well to the Fellowship's expectations for public, observable, reusable work.

**On bridge-building across ecosystems.** Diego's portfolio, Basics Tech (Spanish non-profit), Infinite Labs OÜ (Estonia), Contestia, AGING-AI, Regulia, positions him at the intersection of several conversations that don't usually happen together: healthcare deployment, EU regulatory compliance (AI Act, EAA, NIS2), production open-source development, and payment infrastructure. The AccessPay Fellowship proposal leverages this positioning to do something specific: bring Open Payments into the European non-profit and digital-inclusion sector, using real production systems as deployment grounds. The European third-sector and small-clinic healthcare context is currently underrepresented in the Interledger Foundation's funded portfolio, which concentrates in North America and Africa. As someone inside the Spanish public healthcare sector, I can confirm that this intersection is genuinely underserved and that the work Diego is proposing has no European counterpart today.

**On the AGING-AI pilot specifically.** AGING-AI is a co-funded digital-inclusion pilot for 50 elderly users in the Vallès Occidental, running mid to late 2026 with DKV Impacta 2026 support. I've followed the pilot's design and I see in it the same qualities the Fellowship is asking for: a concrete deployment, a real vulnerable user population, and a clear path from an AI-assisted interface to tangible improvements in access to public services. Plugging Open Payments into a platform like this, as Diego proposes, is the kind of concrete experimentation the Interledger Foundation's portfolio benefits from: it tests whether the rails are ready for elderly, non-technical users, and it returns honest findings either way.

**On the research dimension.** The Fellowship proposal includes a qualitative study of 10 to 15 European third-sector entities. Diego has the network (Basics Tech, DKV partnerships, Vallès municipalities, regional healthcare organisations including ours) and the operational patience to do this properly, including the ethics and consent work that qualitative research with non-profit partners requires. From where I sit, this part of the proposal is the piece most likely to produce findings that no other applicant could produce, because it depends on a trust network that takes years to build.

**On character.** In our interactions I've found Diego to be candid, well-organised, realistic about what he can and cannot deliver, and genuinely driven by the mission of bringing technology to groups that are usually left outside the conversation. He is the right kind of person for a Fellowship that depends on the fellow carrying a sustained load of research, technical, and community work across twelve months.

I recommend Diego without reservation for the 2026 Interledger Fellowship.

Sincerely,

Dr. Alberto Zamora, MD, PhD
