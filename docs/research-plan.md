# Research plan

Research component of the AccessPay fellowship, roughly 14 hours per week (about 45% of the 32-hour week). Paired with [proposal.md](proposal.md).

## Research question

Where can Open Payments create value in the European non-profit and digital-inclusion sector, what's blocking that today, and what would need to change (spec, tooling, policy, infrastructure) for those flows to be viable?

## Shape

A qualitative, mixed-methods study over 12 months. Semi-structured interviews, archive analysis, and case studies. Not a survey, not a quantitative benchmark. The goal is depth across a curated set of 10 to 15 entities, not breadth.

## Entity shortlist

Target: 10 to 15 entities, spanning three categories, at least two non-Spanish to avoid single-country bias.

**A. Municipal digital-inclusion programmes**
- Ajuntament de Terrassa (digital inclusion for seniors and migrants)
- Ajuntament de Sabadell
- 2 to 3 additional Vallès Occidental municipalities
- At least one non-Spanish equivalent (candidate: Barcelona Activa, or a Berlin or Amsterdam digital inclusion initiative)

**B. Health and care non-profits**
- ASICRE (Spanish caregivers association, current partner)
- 1 or 2 small regional clinic networks that work with Contestia
- A patient-association network (FEDER, EURORDIS)

**C. NGO umbrella groups and digital-rights bodies**
- Oxfam Intermón (cross-border transfers)
- NLnet (open standards funder, EU scope)
- Eticas or Algorithm Watch (digital rights in Europe)
- 1 or 2 small fundraising platforms that currently use closed-source gateways

## Method

**Phase 1 (months 1-2):** framing. Literature review of Open Payments, GNAP, and prior Interledger Foundation research. Read Sheena Allen's Southern US study and extract methodology. Draft interview guide (semi-structured, 45 to 60 minutes, 12 to 15 questions per entity). Ethics and consent templates. Pre-registered study design on the public repo.

**Phase 2 (months 3-8):** fieldwork in three waves of 5 entities each.
- Wave 1 (months 3-4): warm contacts via Basics Tech and DKV Impacta partners. Lower risk, lets me calibrate the interview guide.
- Wave 2 (months 5-6): broader Spanish and one non-Spanish. Test the guide in a different context.
- Wave 3 (months 7-8): pan-European, at least 2 non-Spanish. Saturation check.

After each wave I pause for 1 to 2 weeks of analysis and member-checking (sharing draft case sketches with interviewees for corrections).

**Phase 3 (months 9-11):** analysis and drafting. Thematic coding across all transcripts. Cross-case comparison. Draft the findings paper. Peer review by 2 to 3 people in the Interledger community and 2 to 3 in the Spanish third sector.

**Phase 4 (month 12):** publication and dissemination. English + Spanish versions. Creative Commons BY-SA license. Hosted on the fellowship repo, BasicsTech.org, and (if the Foundation is open to it) the Interledger community forum.

## Outputs

- A findings paper, roughly 8,000 to 12,000 words, structured around the research question. English and Spanish versions.
- Per-entity case sketches (anonymised where requested). Each 500 to 1,200 words.
- A public map, as a simple diagram, of where Open Payments fits in the European third-sector payments stack today, and where it doesn't yet.
- A set of recommendations: for the Interledger Foundation (what to fund next), for the Open Payments spec (what gaps surfaced), for the European third sector (what to adopt and when).

## Ethics

- Informed consent on all interviews.
- Participants can review their case sketches before publication and request anonymisation or redaction.
- No personal data about beneficiaries of the interviewed entities. Only operational and strategic information at the entity level.
- Interview recordings kept encrypted and deleted after the study ends.
- Consent template drafted in month 1, posted publicly in the repo.

## What this isn't

- Not a survey. I'm not chasing statistical representativeness. A curated 10 to 15 is the right size for the question.
- Not advocacy in disguise. The paper will name things that don't work, including in Open Payments itself if that's what the interviews surface.
- Not a technical deliverable. The technical work happens in parallel (see [technical-plan.md](technical-plan.md)) and is different in nature.
