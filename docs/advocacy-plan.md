# Advocacy plan

Advocacy and community component of the AccessPay fellowship, roughly 5 hours per week (about 15% of the 32-hour week). Paired with [proposal.md](proposal.md).

The goal is narrow: connect the Interledger ecosystem with the European impact-focused non-profit ecosystem, using the fellowship's research and technical work as the bridge. Two talks, one written guide, some community plumbing.

## Two conference talks

### Talk 1: "Open Payments in the European third sector, what we learned"

Primary target: **FOSDEM 2027** (Brussels, early February), Open Source / Payments / Web Monetization devroom.
Backup targets: **NLnet/NGI annual gathering** (EU-focused, fits the audience), **Froscon**, **State of the Map Europe**.

Content: findings-paper highlights, honest notes on where Open Payments fits today and where it doesn't, short demo of the Contestia integration. 25 to 30 minutes. Slides public on the repo under CC-BY-SA.

Submission window: FOSDEM 2027 CFP typically opens September 2026. I draft the submission in month 6, file it in month 7 or 8.

### Talk 2: "Integrating Open Payments into production health SaaS, honest notes"

Primary target: **Open Source Summit Europe 2026** (October).
Backup targets: **Interledger Summit** (if there's a 2026 edition with a fitting track), a European healthtech conference, a Spanish-language NGO-tech gathering.

Content: the Contestia integration in detail, from architectural decision to production rollout. Developer-focused, not advocacy-heavy. Covers the real questions: how did you feature-flag it, what broke, what's the ROI for a real clinic. 25 to 30 minutes.

Submission window: OSSEU 2026 CFP opens around May 2026. I'd need to submit on the back of the month 6 post-mortem.

## Public practitioner's guide

Title (working): **"Integrating Open Payments in production impact software: a practitioner's guide"**.

Length: 3,000 to 5,000 words, English and Spanish versions.

Scope: a hands-on walkthrough of the Contestia and AGING-AI integrations, minus the hype. How to set up a Rafiki sandbox, how to pick a wallet provider, how to feature-flag a new payment rail behind an existing one, how to handle the KYC and compliance questions for small EU non-profits, how to decide if Open Payments is ready for your specific use case (and when it isn't).

Hosted on:
- The fellowship repo, `docs/guide/`
- BasicsTech.org as a long-form post
- Mirrored on the Interledger community forum if the foundation is open to it

Creative Commons BY-SA license, same as the research paper.

Timing: drafted in months 10 and 11, published in month 11 or early month 12 alongside the findings paper.

## Community plumbing

Not a deliverable, but a constant throughout the fellowship:

- Monthly progress notes in #cfp-sdk and on the Interledger community forum. Short, one or two paragraphs, linking to whatever shipped that month.
- Active in the Interledger community forum when newcomers ask about EU third-sector use cases.
- If the fellowship generates warm contacts between Interledger folks and the European non-profit folks I'm interviewing, introduce them. Not a deliverable but the most valuable thing that could come out of 12 months of sustained presence in both ecosystems.

## What this isn't

- Not a marketing campaign. No paid promotion, no glossy content.
- Not lobbying. The European third sector has its own advocacy channels; I'm not trying to duplicate them.
- Not a standalone programme. The advocacy track only works because the research and technical tracks produce something worth advocating for.
